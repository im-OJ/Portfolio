Dota 2 Custom Games

My biggest success (user-wise) to date was a custom game I built for Dota 2. As soon as the tools were released to developers I jumped in and tried to figure things out. The programming here was done in Lua. This was the first time I was learning to program with a tool that did not have a huge community and only limited documentation online. To me this was a very valuable learning experience. The custom game is not played anymore however the days following its released it amassed thousands of players. Today it sits at 75,900 downloads, with over 22,000 ratings averaging 4/5 stars. The page on the steam workshop for the game can be seen here.

More WebDev

In my spare time at uni I also created a couple of small sites. One of which would take a direct link to an audio file and create a page where said file can be streamed, and the submitter could view statistics about who was streaming their song.
Another project was a lottery for the cryptocurrency Nano. Users bought tickets by sending crypto to an address on the site, tickets simply contained the address and how many tickets the amount they sent was worth - no sign up system. One address would be randomly selected at the end and the majority of money the site had been sent would be given to that winner.
I never publically announced the site as I was afraid of possible legal repercussions and insecurities in the site.
